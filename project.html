Hypothesis

Formulation

Employees with higher salaries complete more projects and have a higher average efficiency score

Hypothesis check

Average number of projects and efficiency score calculated by salary level.
Results displayed in a grouped bar chart.
In[38]:
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

salary_analysis = emp_ad.groupby('salary').agg(
    avg_projects=('number_project', 'mean'),
    avg_evaluation=('last_evaluation', 'mean')
).reset_index()

salary_analysis.plot(
    x='salary', 
    kind='bar', 
    figsize=(10, 6), 
    color=['skyblue', 'salmon'], 
    legend=True
)

plt.title('Comparison of Average Projects and Efficiency Score by Salary Level', fontsize=16)
plt.xlabel('Salary Level', fontsize=14)
plt.ylabel('Average Values', fontsize=14)
plt.legend(['Avg. Projects', 'Avg. Efficiency Score'], title='Metrics')
plt.grid(axis='y', linestyle='--', alpha=0.7)

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Conclusion

As we can see a high salary does not affect performance, different salaries show the same average values
